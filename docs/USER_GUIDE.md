# DealSim User Guide

**Your personal negotiation coach. Practice, learn, and walk into your next negotiation ready.**

---

## Welcome to DealSim

DealSim is a negotiation training simulator. Think of it as a flight simulator, but instead of landing a plane, you're landing a better salary, a lower rent, or a fair deal on a car.

Here's what you get:

- **Safe practice space.** Negotiate against a calibrated AI opponent without any real-world consequences. Make mistakes, try bold moves, experiment freely.
- **Instant feedback.** After every session, you receive a detailed scorecard that breaks your performance down across six research-backed dimensions.
- **A personalized playbook.** DealSim generates a cheat sheet tailored to your specific situation — complete with exact phrases to use and traps to avoid.
- **Real confidence.** The more you practice, the more natural negotiation feels. Most people find that even two or three sessions change how they approach real conversations.

Whether you're negotiating your first job offer, pushing back on a medical bill, or buying a car, DealSim helps you prepare with intention instead of winging it.

## The Negotiation Simulator

This is the core of DealSim. The full simulator gives you complete control over the scenario, the difficulty, and the opponent's personality.

### Step 1: Choose Your Scenario

On the home screen, scroll down to the **Full Simulation Setup** card. Select your scenario type from the dropdown:

| Scenario | You're negotiating... |
|----------|----------------------|
| **Salary Negotiation** | Base pay for a new job offer |
| **Freelance Rate** | Your hourly or project rate with a client |
| **Business Deal** | Terms of a business agreement |
| **Custom** | Anything else — describe it in the context field |

[Screenshot: Scenario type dropdown expanded]

### Step 2: Set Your Target

Enter your **target outcome** — the number you're aiming for. For a salary negotiation, this might be $95,000. For a freelance rate, it could be $150/hour.

This helps DealSim calibrate the simulation. The AI opponent won't know your target, but the scoring engine uses it to evaluate how well you performed.

### Step 3: Pick a Difficulty

Choose from three levels:

- **Easy** — The opponent is collaborative and patient. Good for learning the basics.
- **Medium** — A realistic negotiation partner. Pushes back but can be persuaded.
- **Hard** — The opponent uses pressure tactics, hides information, and doesn't give ground easily.

Start with Easy if this is your first time. Move to Hard when you want a real challenge.

### Step 4: Describe the Situation

In the **"Describe the Situation"** text box, give the AI context about your negotiation. The more detail you provide, the more realistic the simulation becomes.

Good examples:
- "I have 3 years of backend engineering experience and received an offer of $85k from a Series B startup in Austin."
- "I'm a freelance designer with 5 years of experience. The client wants a full brand identity package and offered $3,000."

### Step 5: Tune Your Opponent (Optional)

Click **"Tune Opponent Persona"** to open the Opponent Tuner. This screen has six sliders that control how the AI behaves:

| Slider | What It Controls |
|--------|-----------------|
| **Aggressiveness** | Friendly (0) to Hardball (100). High values mean the opponent pushes hard. |
| **Flexibility** | Rigid (0) to Very Flexible (100). How willing they are to make concessions. |
| **Patience** | Impatient (0) to Very Patient (100). Low patience means pressure to close quickly. |
| **Market Knowledge** | Uninformed (0) to Expert (100). An expert opponent will call out unrealistic asks. |
| **Emotional Reactivity** | Stoic (0) to Highly Reactive (100). High reactivity means they may get frustrated or excited. |
| **Budget Authority** | No Authority (0) to Full Authority (100). Low authority means they'll say "I need to check with my manager." |

Hover over any slider label to see a tooltip with more detail.

When you're done, click **"Apply & Setup Sim"** to return to the setup screen with your tuning saved.

[Screenshot: Opponent Tuner with sliders at various positions]

### Step 6: Start Negotiating

Click **"Start Negotiation."** You'll enter the chat interface.

**The chat screen shows:**
- The opponent's name and role in the top bar
- The current round number
- An "End & Score" button when you're ready to wrap up

**Tips for effective practice:**
- **Open strong.** State your number first when possible — this is called anchoring.
- **Ask questions.** "What's the budget range for this role?" counts as an information-gathering move and improves your score.
- **Reference alternatives.** Mentioning other offers or options (your BATNA) gives you leverage.
- **Make small concessions.** Moving from $100k to $95k is better than jumping from $100k to $85k.
- **Stay calm.** Even if the opponent pressures you, measured responses score higher on emotional control.

**Keyboard shortcuts:**
- `Enter` — send your message
- `Shift+Enter` — new line (without sending)
- `Esc` — end the negotiation and get your score

When you're ready to finish, click **"End & Score"** or press Escape.

[Screenshot: Chat interface mid-negotiation with opponent and user messages]

### Understanding Your Scorecard

After the negotiation ends, you'll see your **DealSim Score** — a number from 0 to 100 displayed in a large circle at the top of the screen.

Below that, the **Breakdown** section shows your performance across six dimensions:

| Dimension | Weight | What It Measures |
|-----------|--------|-----------------|
| **Opening Strategy** | 20% | Did you anchor first? Was your opening ambitious enough? |
| **Information Gathering** | 15% | Did you ask questions to uncover the opponent's constraints and priorities? |
| **Concession Pattern** | 25% | Were your concessions small and decelerating? (Giving less each time signals you're near your limit.) |
| **BATNA Usage** | 15% | Did you reference alternatives to create leverage? |
| **Emotional Control** | 10% | Did you stay composed when the opponent pushed back or used pressure tactics? |
| **Value Creation** | 15% | Did you explore creative solutions beyond just the headline number? (e.g., signing bonus, start date, equity) |

Each dimension gets a score from 0-100, shown as a color-coded progress bar.

Below the breakdown, you'll find **Coaching Tips** — specific, actionable advice based on what the AI observed in your negotiation.

## What They Were Thinking (Debrief)

This is where DealSim gets really valuable. After you see your scorecard, click the **"Debrief & Reveal"** button to find out what was going on inside the opponent's head.

### The Opponent's Hidden State

During the negotiation, the AI had information you didn't see:
- **Reservation price** — the absolute best deal they were willing to give you
- **Hidden constraints** — things like budget limits, timeline pressure, or approval requirements
- **Pressure felt** — how much your moves actually shifted their position

The debrief reveals all of this.

### Money Left on the Table

This is the headline number in the debrief. It shows the gap between what you achieved and what was theoretically possible.

For example, if you agreed on $92,000 but the opponent's hidden reservation price was $98,000, you left $6,000 on the table.

The rating tells you how much of the available value you captured:
- **90%+** — Excellent. You pushed close to their limit.
- **70-89%** — Good. Solid performance with some room for improvement.
- **Below 70%** — Room to improve. The debrief will show you where the opportunity was.

### Move-by-Move Analysis

Each exchange is broken down with the opponent's internal state at that moment:
- What they were thinking
- How close you were to triggering acceptance
- How much pressure they felt from your specific move

This is the fastest way to learn. You'll see exactly which of your moves worked and which ones didn't.

### How to Use This to Improve

1. Look at the move where pressure was highest — that's your strongest tactic. Use it more.
2. Look at the move where you made the biggest concession — that's where you gave away the most value.
3. Check the "key moments" section for turning points you might have missed.

## Your Negotiation Playbook

After completing a negotiation, click the **"Playbook"** button on the scorecard to generate a personalized cheat sheet.

### What the Playbook Contains

- **Your Negotiation Style** — a summary of how you tend to approach negotiations based on this session
- **Strengths** — what you did well (so you keep doing it)
- **Areas to Improve** — specific weaknesses with concrete advice
- **Recommendations** — tactical suggestions you can apply immediately
- **Practice Scenarios** — suggested follow-up simulations to target your weak areas

### How to Use It Before a Real Negotiation

The playbook is designed to be your preparation tool. Here's the workflow:

1. Run a simulation that matches your real situation (same role, same salary range, similar context).
2. Generate the playbook.
3. **Print it** (click the Print button in the top-right corner) or **copy it to your clipboard** (click Copy).
4. Review it 30 minutes before your real negotiation.
5. Focus on the recommendations section — those are the moves most likely to improve your outcome.

## Offer Analyzer

The Offer Analyzer is a standalone tool — you don't need to run a simulation first. Use it whenever you receive an offer and want to know where you stand.

### How to Use It

1. Click **"Offer Analyzer"** from the home screen (or the "Offers" link in the navigation bar).
2. Fill in the details:
   - **Your Role / Title** — e.g., "Senior Software Engineer"
   - **Base Salary Offered** — the headline number
   - **Signing Bonus** — if applicable
   - **Equity / Stock** — annual value if applicable
   - **Location** — this affects market comparisons
   - **Full Offer Details** — paste the entire offer letter or describe the complete package (PTO, benefits, remote policy, etc.)
3. Click **"Analyze Offer."**

### Understanding the Results

The analysis shows:

- **Market Position** — where your offer falls relative to market benchmarks (below market, at market, above market)
- **Negotiability Scores** — which parts of the offer are most likely to be negotiable
- **Red Flags** — things missing from the offer that you should ask about
- **Recommendations** — specific advice on what to negotiate and how much room you likely have

### Using the Counter-Offer Strategies

Below the analysis, you'll see the option to **"Practice Negotiating This Offer."** This takes your offer details and drops them straight into a simulation, so you can rehearse the actual conversation before having it for real.

## Daily Challenges

Daily Challenges are bite-sized negotiation exercises that take about 3 minutes each.

### What They Are

Each day, DealSim presents a new micro-scenario with a specific constraint. These are short (typically 3 exchanges) and focus on a single negotiation skill.

### The 6 Tactic Categories

Challenges rotate through these categories:

1. **Anchoring** — Practice setting the first number to frame the negotiation
2. **Information Gathering** — Get what you need before making concessions
3. **Concession Strategy** — Give ground strategically, not emotionally
4. **BATNA Leverage** — Use your alternatives effectively
5. **Emotional Control** — Stay composed under pressure
6. **Value Creation** — Find creative solutions that expand the pie

### Building a Practice Habit

The daily challenge is designed to be quick enough that you can do it during a coffee break. Consistency beats intensity — doing one challenge per day for two weeks builds more skill than one marathon session.

Check back each day for a new challenge. The date is displayed on the challenge card so you know when a fresh one is available.

## Score History

Every time you complete a negotiation (including demos and daily challenges), your score is saved to your browser's local storage.

### Tracking Your Improvement

Navigate to **"History"** in the top navigation bar to see a chart of your scores over time.

- The **line chart** shows your overall score progression
- Below the chart, a **list of past sessions** shows the date, scenario type, and score for each one

### What the Trend Means

- **Rising line** — You're improving. Keep practicing.
- **Flat line** — You may be plateauing. Try increasing the difficulty or focusing on a weaker dimension.
- **Dip after difficulty increase** — Completely normal. Hard opponents expose gaps that Easy opponents don't.

If you want to start fresh, there's a "Clear all history" link at the bottom of the page.

## Tools

### Lifetime Earnings Calculator

This tool shows the compounding impact of negotiating a higher salary over the course of your career.

**How to use it:**

1. Click **"Earnings Calc"** on the home screen or **"Calculator"** in the navigation bar.
2. Enter your **current/offered salary** (what they offered you).
3. Enter your **negotiated salary** (what you think you can get).
4. Set the **years remaining in your career** and your expected **annual raise percentage**.
5. The results update instantly as you type.

**What it shows:**
- **Year 1 difference** — the immediate annual impact
- **Year 10 difference** — the cumulative impact after a decade of compounding raises
- **Lifetime difference** — the total career impact

The numbers can be eye-opening. A $10,000 salary increase negotiated today can compound into $200,000+ over a 30-year career with 3% annual raises.

## Tips for Getting the Most Out of DealSim

### 1. Practice before a REAL negotiation

DealSim is most valuable when you use it to prepare for something specific. Got a job offer coming? Run 3-4 simulations with matching context before the call. The rehearsal makes a measurable difference.

### 2. Try different opponents

Use the Opponent Tuner to simulate different personalities. Your real counterpart might be a friendly HR manager (low aggressiveness, high flexibility) or a tough CFO (high aggressiveness, low flexibility, expert market knowledge). Practice for both.

### 3. Focus on one dimension at a time

If your scorecard shows 85 on Opening Strategy but 35 on Information Gathering, don't try to fix everything at once. Run your next session with the specific goal of asking more questions. Watch that one score climb.

### 4. Share your score and challenge friends

After any session, click **"Share Your Score"** on the scorecard. Compare scores with colleagues or friends who are also preparing for negotiations. A little friendly competition keeps practice interesting.

### 5. Use the playbook in the real meeting

Print the playbook or copy it to your phone. Glance at it before the meeting starts. Having specific phrases ready ("Based on market data for this role in this region...") makes you sound prepared and confident — because you are.

### 6. Run the Offer Analyzer before you simulate

If you have a real offer, analyze it first to understand where you stand in the market. Then use "Practice Negotiating This Offer" to rehearse with that specific context loaded into the simulation.

### 7. Do the daily challenge

Three minutes a day keeps your negotiation instincts sharp. It's the difference between someone who negotiates once a year (nervously) and someone who practices regularly (confidently).

## FAQ

**Is my data private?**
Yes. All your session data, scores, and history are stored locally in your browser. DealSim does not use third-party tracking or analytics services. Your negotiation transcripts are processed by the server for scoring and then not retained beyond your session.

**Can I use this for non-salary negotiations?**
Absolutely. DealSim supports salary, freelance rate, and business deal scenarios out of the box. You can also select "Custom" and describe any negotiation situation — rent negotiations, medical bills, car purchases, contractor rates, or anything else.

**How accurate is the scoring?**
The six scoring dimensions are based on established negotiation research. The AI evaluates concrete, observable behaviors: Did you anchor? Did you ask questions? Did your concessions decelerate? This isn't subjective judgment — it's pattern detection applied to your specific moves.

**Can I practice with a friend?**
Not yet. Multiplayer negotiation practice is on the roadmap. For now, you can share your scores and challenge friends to beat them on the same scenario.

**Does DealSim work on mobile?**
Yes. The interface is fully responsive. The chat interface, scorecard, and all tools work on phones and tablets.

**How is the "Money Left on the Table" number calculated?**
It compares the final agreed value against the opponent's hidden reservation price (the best deal they were secretly willing to accept). The gap between those two numbers is the money you left on the table.

**Can I go back and review a past negotiation?**
Your score history is preserved, but individual transcripts are not stored long-term. If you want to review a specific negotiation, take a screenshot of the debrief before leaving the session.

**What's the best way to improve quickly?**
Run 3 simulations in a row on the same scenario, increasing difficulty each time. After each one, read the debrief carefully. Most people see a 15-20 point score improvement within their first three sessions.
